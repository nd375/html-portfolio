ND Watches Movies Sometimes

and sometimes they like them

1. Spiderman: Into the Spiderverse

The only thing to not like about this movie is that Miles' dad is a cop and that's unfortunate. But everything else is sublime. The animation is gorgeous. The dialogue is funny. The characters are lovable. The character designs go hard af. I had a hard time choosing between this one and the sequel because boy do I love Hobie Brown and Pavitr Prabhakar. And Miguel O'Hara so sorry for forgetting you babe. But this one is just such a classic. Genre-shifting.

2. It's a Wonderful Life

Idk if I actually like this movie this much or I was just exposed to it so much that it has wormed into the good graces corner of my brain. We watched it every year at Christmas time when I was growing up. And you know what, it has some d*mn good themes about the evils of capitalism and the importance of community work, so. Plus Jimmy Stewart is like an icon or something I guess. A classic. Heartwarming, entertaining classic.

3. The Emperor's New Groove

Yes. Yes. Just...yes. I mean, Kronk. Need I say more? But I will. Because not only are the vocal performances totally on point (shoutout Eartha Kitt rip you were an icon and a goddess) and the animation great - the story is good, original, funny, and some of those lines of dialogue/scenes are eternally and I mean eternally quotable. See, for example, "I never liked your spinach puffs!" or maybe "Why do we even have that lever?" or a little "By all accounts it doesn't make sense." Bromance at its finest, great music, yeah, it's a good one.
